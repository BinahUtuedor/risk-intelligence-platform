# Business Problem – Risk Intelligence Platform

## Overview

Global financial institutions, compliance teams, and risk analysts must continuously evaluate countries and entities for exposure to:

- Economic instability
- Weak governance structures
- Corruption risk
- Regulatory risk
- Sanctions exposure
- Politically Exposed Person (PEP) risk

Manual screening processes are slow, inconsistent, and difficult to scale.

---

## Problem Statement

There is a need for an automated, explainable system that:

1. Aggregates global risk indicators
2. Flags high-risk jurisdictions
3. Combines sanctions and governance signals
4. Produces a unified risk score
5. Prioritises entities for investigation

---

## Objectives

The Risk Intelligence Platform aims to:

- Build a unified country-level risk dataset
- Integrate external data (World Bank indicators)
- Apply rule-based compliance screening
- Train a machine learning model for risk classification
- Generate explainable risk scores
- Provide executive-level reporting outputs

---

## Business Value

### 1. Faster Compliance Screening
Reduces manual investigation time by automating initial risk classification.

### 2. Consistent Risk Scoring
Ensures standardised evaluation across all countries.

### 3. Improved Regulatory Compliance
Supports AML, KYC, and sanctions screening workflows.

### 4. Explainability
Provides interpretable features for audit and regulatory review.

---

## Target Users

- Compliance Officers
- Risk Analysts
- AML/KYC Teams
- Financial Crime Units
- Data Science Teams in Risk Departments

---

## Key Output

- Risk score per country/entity
- Binary risk classification (`risk_flag`)
- Executive summary metrics
- Feature importance for transparency
